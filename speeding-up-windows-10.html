Speeding Up Windows 10

Preamble

Depending on who you ask, Windows 10 is either the best operating system to date, or the worst hunk of garbage that has ever graced their computer.

It’s really not hard to see why the reception of this operating system is so polarized: Windows 10 has brought about so many great features like multiple desktops (something Linux users has had for two decades), improved driver autodetection,  Continuum (for those with hybrid devices), a simplified/unified settings panel that helps out 10% of the time, and a bunch more I couldn’t care less about.

On the flip side, Windows 10 (actually, since Windows 7) brought about forced updates and reboots, and a steady stream of additional features that, instead of helping out the user keep the system in perfect working order or assist in the most menial of tasks, hammered the operating system disk with non-essential background tasks that kept reading and writing to drive c.

These updates essentially make Windows 10 unusable on systems with slow storage. 100% disk usage makes the whole desktop and every other application unresponsive as fuck, and updates either hammer the disk usage themselves while downloading or take 45 minutes to an hour to install packages. For the latter case, I even witnessed that happen at work when I was still employed at some cheapskate marketing company.

The fix

Jesus Christ. Just get a fucking SSD.

Whether you are on a laptop or a desktop, or whether you have a spare drive slot or not, there is simply no excuse for not having an SSD installed.

240GB SSDs are fucking cheap. If you’re on a laptop and you need large storage capacities, external hard drive enclosures are also fucking cheap – just slap your laptop’s old hard drive in there and you’re good to go. Stop wasting your time waiting for Windows to “start being ready to launch apps”. Stop wasting your time waiting for the Downloads folder to complete listing its contents. Stop wasting your time trying to figure out why the fuck your laptop is running extremely hot, even though you have reinstalled Windows a fuck-ton of times and are absolutely sure you’re not running any bloatware whatsoever.

Online guides will tell you to turn off all visual effects, run disk cleanup, defragment, disable startup programs, turn off Cortana and search indexing, and all sorts of bullshit. Those won’t do jack, because (a) your problem is not the lack of processing power, and (b) these services don’t gobble up disk usage as much as you’d think.

Even if you’re rocking an i69s-6969K your anemic hard drive will hold you back. The CPU simply can’t do shit if it can’t get the data off your hard drive. No OS reinstall will fix that.

240GB SSDs are 25 USD. How much is your time worth, really? Is your time really worth being spent on following some guy’s tutorial on the internet, whose sole motivation “to help you” is ad revenue from the ads and trackers peppered across his site?

Give it a shot. Get an SSD and install it yourself or have someone do it for you. Bam. Problems solved.

Spreading the word

Let’s assume that someone actually came into my blog, read this post, and changed his mind and got himself an SSD. He’s probably so stoked about how much a small credit card-sized thing fixed all his problems with his computer that he wants to tell all his friends about it. He’d want to preach the word of the SSD, and how it will save us from the evil productivity killer called the mechanical hard drive.

If you’re someone like the above hypothetical guy, I’m going to tell you straight away that there is no point trying to get people to switch to SSDs. They simply won’t care – even if they do, it’s extremely hard to quantify the performance gains of an SSD. It’s not like this is a processor where you can give it a core count or a clock speed to compare speeds with – storage options are being discussed in terms of capacity and not much else. Ah yes, capacity – good luck getting past the “capacity downgrade” argument that is always raised during these conversations.

So unless you’re head of IT or a tech-savvy business operations manager who gives a shit about office productivity, just shut it and enjoy watching other people struggle getting shit done on HDDs.

You’re not changing anyone’s minds. Hell, even performance comparisons between SSDs and HDDs on YouTube have been received positively only by those already on SSDs. The results have been completely ignored by those “smart enough not to buy something overpriced that holds much fewer data”.

Much like everything else, storage capacity discussions are avenue for dick-measuring contests. By going with SSDs, you’ll have the smaller dick, and nobody really wants that - even if you’re able to thrust magnitudes faster than the other bros.
